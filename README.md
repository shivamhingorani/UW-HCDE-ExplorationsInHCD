# HCDE 210: Explorations in Human Centered Design

**Institution:** University of Washington  
**Term:** Winter 2019

---

## About the Course

HCDE 210 is a broad survey of the human-centered design process — an introduction to the methods, mindsets, and materials that practitioners use to understand people and design things for them. Rather than focusing on any single design domain, the course is organized as a series of modules, each of which places students in a different part of the HCD landscape: studying users in the field, generating ideas, building interactive prototypes, constructing physical artifacts, testing designs with real users, and making data visible.

Running parallel to the design modules is a thread of reflective and ethical practice. Weekly reflections push toward articulating *why* a design decision was made, not just *what* was made; the Responsible Practice articles situate design work in a broader social and professional context, asking what designers owe to the people they design for.

The cumulative arc of the course is the HCD cycle — understand, ideate, prototype, evaluate — visited from multiple angles and at multiple levels of fidelity.

---

## Topics Covered

### User Research
The course opens with user research as the foundation of human-centered practice. The deliverable covers primary research methods — observation, interviews, and contextual inquiry — and the process of synthesizing raw field data into insight. The focus is on understanding users in their own contexts rather than projecting assumptions onto them.

### Ideation
The ideation module explores methods for generating a large space of design possibilities before converging on any particular solution. Work in this module spans concept sketching, brainstorming, and structured divergent thinking techniques. The deliverable represents a process of expanding options before narrowing — a discipline in resisting premature closure.

### Interaction Design
The interaction design module moves from ideas to interactive form, producing a prototype of a digital interface. The deliverable demonstrates an understanding of interaction patterns, flow, and the moment-to-moment experience of using a designed system. Video documentation captures the prototype in motion rather than as a static artifact.

### Physical Computing
The physical computing module shifts to tangible, hardware-based interaction — designing and building an artifact that responds to physical input or produces physical output. Video documentation shows the artifact operating in a real context. The module introduces the idea that interaction design extends beyond screens and software.

### Usability Testing
The usability testing module applies evaluation methods to an existing design: recruiting participants, designing tasks, facilitating sessions, and analyzing results. The deliverable captures the gap between how designers imagine their work will be used and how it is actually used — a gap that is the practical motivation for the entire HCD process.

### Information Visualization
The information visualization module covers how to represent data so that patterns and relationships become visible. The deliverable applies encoding principles — choosing the right visual variables for the data type — and produces a visualization designed to communicate a specific insight rather than merely display raw data.

### Reflective Practice
Woven through the course are four reflective writing assignments — an orientation reflection before the design modules begin and three reflections tracking how understanding developed across the quarter, culminating in an end-of-quarter synthesis.

### Responsible Practice
The Responsible Practice track consists of three short articles and a final synthesis, each engaging with the ethical, social, and professional dimensions of design work: who is included and excluded by design decisions, what designers owe to users and communities, and how responsible practice is maintained in professional contexts.

## Methods and Tools

| Method / Tool | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Contextual inquiry / interviews | User research: understanding people in context |
| Concept sketching | Ideation: divergent generation of design possibilities |
| Interactive prototyping | Interaction design: simulating digital experiences |
| Physical computing | Tangible interaction: hardware-based artifact design |
| Usability testing | Evaluation: structured observation of design in use |
| Information visualization | Data communication: encoding data to reveal patterns |
| Reflective writing | Metacognitive practice: articulating design rationale |
